# Business Problem
Local farmers often struggle with reliable delivery of crops to markets. Trusted delivery hubs often do not have available routes through rural regions, causing food distribution inequity.

# Solution
A platform for connecting farmers to delivery hubs (e.g. Roadrunner Food Bank). Delivery hubs would plan an initial round-trip delivery route with a larger entity (e.g. government services) that takes a route through rural areas. On the trip back, delivery trucks would pick up food waste to deliver to farmers for composting. Local farmers in proximity would be notified and have an open invitation to reach out and coordinate with the delivery hub to have their goods picked up on the way and delivered at target destinations (schools, restaurants, markets). It would support integration of their existing systems through custom development. For farmers, interactions with the applications should be minimal and low-tech if possible (e.g. SMS). For delivery hubs, they should be able to plan out deliveries using a GIS-like experience. 

# MVP

## Functional Requirements

- For farmers, a text message notifications of opportunities with delivery hubs along with their contact details
- For distribution hubs, a map widget allowing them to set a delivery route between two points. They should be able to configure the title, start time, end time, and notes
- Should use OAuth (or magic email links) to minimize signup friction
